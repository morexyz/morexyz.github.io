Privacy-first audio intelligence at the edge.

A research proposal for an ESP32-based IoT audio agent that captures machine sound, performs on-device inference, and adapts over time without sending raw audio to the cloud.

Problem · Objectives · Research Gap

Why this research matters

Problem

Static embedded audio models degrade when room acoustics, microphone position, machinery aging, or background noise change.

Objectives

Build a full ESP32 audio pipeline and add lightweight on-device adaptation for robust anomaly detection over time.

Research gap

Most embedded audio work proves inference feasibility, but not privacy-preserving long-term adaptation under drift.

System Architecture

Audio capture, inference, and safe adaptation on device

Stabilization

VAD gating, running normalization, and conservative thresholds reduce noisy updates.

Learning

A compact prototype in embedding space updates only on high-confidence normal segments, with rollback protection.

Evaluation & Timeline

Measure robustness, resources, and real-world usefulness

Metrics

AUROC, false alarms per hour, latency, RAM/flash, energy per inference, and adaptation settling time.

Conditions

Noise variation, microphone placement changes, abrupt environment shifts, and gradual drift.

Data

MIMII, ToyADMOS, DCASE Task 2 ASD, plus a self-collected BLDC fan dataset.
